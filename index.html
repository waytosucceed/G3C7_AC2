Vowels


Overview of Grammar topics:

Short vowels are those that appear individually in words. These words normally end with consonants.

For example: "met," "sit," "dot," and "mug."

A long vowel sound is one where the vowel's sound is pronounced for an extended period, or "held out" for a longer duration.

For example: tight, eye, road,door, queue.

Vowels are: a, e, i, o, and u.

Consonants are the rest of the letters in the alphabet: b, c, d, f, g, h, j, k, l, m, n, p, q, r, s, t, v, w, x, y, and z.

'r-controlled vowels' have a unique sound that's different from regular short and long vowel sounds.
